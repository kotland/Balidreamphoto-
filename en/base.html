Bali Base

Everything you need to know before your trip

🦎Dangers▼
Snakes
Green vine snake, king cobra. See one — don't touch, back away slowly. Bite → hospital immediately (BIMC, Siloam)
Mosquitoes
Dengue is the main danger. Repellent with DEET, plug-in at night, long sleeves at sunset. Symptoms: 39°C+ fever, body aches → see a doctor
Dogs
Many strays. DON'T TOUCH. Rabies is real. Bitten → wash with soap 15 min → vaccine within 24h
Monkeys
Monkey Forest, temples. Hide glasses, phone, food. Don't smile (showing teeth = aggression)
Currents
Rip current — don't swim against it! Swim parallel to shore. Red flags = no swimming
Jellyfish
Season: October-April. Sting → vinegar or hot water. NOT fresh water
Sun
SPF 50+ required. You'll burn in 20 min. Hat, sunglasses, reapply every 2h
🛵Motorbike▼
Rental
700K-1.5M/month. Honda Vario/Scoopy is standard. Check brakes, lights, tires
License
International driving permit (IDP) required. Without one — 100-500K fine on the spot
Rules
Left-hand traffic! Helmet mandatory. Don't speed — narrow roads, dogs, ceremonies
Grab/Gojek
Alternative to bikes. Ride from 15K, food delivery. Download both apps
💊First Aid Kit▼
Bring from home
Antihistamines, activated charcoal/Pepto, ibuprofen, antiseptic, band-aids, electrolytes
Buy in Bali
Pharmacies: Guardian, Kimia Farma — have everything. Antibiotics without prescription
Repellent
DEET 30%+ (against dengue). Soffell is local, works well. Plug-in at night — MANDATORY
📱Mobile & Internet▼
SIM card
Telkomsel (best coverage), XL, Indosat. Buy at airport or store. 50-100K for 10-20GB
eSIM
Airalo, Holafly — activate before arrival. Convenient but pricier
WhatsApp
The main messenger in Bali. All businesses, drivers, villa owners communicate via WA
💧Water▼
Tap water
DO NOT DRINK! Even brush teeth with bottled water. Seriously.
Gallons
Aqua 19L ~20K ($1.50). Order with delivery. Bottles 5-10K at every store
Ice
In restaurants and cafes — safe (factory-made). At markets — better without
💰Money▼
Currency
Rupiah (IDR). $1 ≈ 15,500. 100K ≈ $6.50. Quick math: remove 4 zeros
Cash
Essential! Small cafes, markets, temples — cash only. Visa works better than Mastercard
Money changers
BMC/BMM — best rates. Don't exchange at airport. Always count when receiving
Bargaining
Required at markets. Start at 30-50% of the asking price
🛡Insurance▼
Mandatory
Must cover motorbike! Without it, a fracture = $5,000-20,000. World Nomads, SafetyWing
What to cover
Hospitalization, evacuation, motorbike, dengue, COVID. Minimum $50,000 coverage
🌧When to Go▼
Dry season
April-October. Best time. 28-32°C, little rain
Wet season
November-March. Rain 1-2h/day (usually afternoon). Greener, cheaper, fewer people
Temperature
28-33°C year-round. Water 27-29°C. Cooler evenings in highlands (Ubud, Kintamani)
🧳What to Pack▼
Documents
Passport (6+ months valid), insurance, IDP, cloud copies of everything
Clothing
Light, breathable. Sarong (or buy locally ~50K). Raincoat. Sneakers for hikes
Tech
Adapter: type C (same as EU). Power bank. Waterproof phone case
Health
SPF 50+, repellent, first aid kit. 2-3 swimsuits (slow drying due to humidity)

🥭Tropical Fruits▼
Mango (Mangga)
Season: October - February.
Balinese mangoes are green on the outside (even when ripe!), but bright orange inside. The sweetest variety is Harum Manis. Choose by smell and softness.
Mangosteen (Manggis)
Season: December - March.
The king of fruits! Thick purple skin, inside - white cloves resembling garlic. The taste is a mix of peach, strawberry, and pineapple. Pick those soft to the touch (hard as rock = spoiled).
Snakefruit (Salak)
Season: Year-round.
The skin looks like snake scales. Inside are crunchy cloves, the taste is a mix of apple, banana, and nuts. Very filling.
Dragonfruit (Buah Naga)
Season: Year-round (peak in winter).
Bright crimson fruit with scales. Inside it can be white or blood-red. Tastes like watery kiwi. Perfectly refreshing in the heat. Stains your hands!
Rambutan
Season: December - March.
Red "hairy" balls. Inside is a juicy pulp (similar to lychee). Sweet like marmalade. Easy to peel by hand.
Passionfruit (Markisa)
Season: Year-round.
Balinese passionfruit is often yellow or greenish, not purple. Inside is a sweet-and-sour jelly-like caviar with seeds.
Papaya
Season: Year-round.
Huge, cheap, perfect for digestion. Buy it already yellow or orange. Green means unripe.
Durian
Season: December - March.
The fruit that smells like hell but tastes like heaven (a mix of custard, onion, and almonds). Banned in hotels. Heats up the body. Do not mix with alcohol!
Soursop (Sirsak)
Season: June - September.
A huge green prickly fruit. Inside is a snow-white pulp with a taste of strawberry, pineapple, and citrus with sour cream. Perfect for smoothies.
Jackfruit (Nangka)
Season: April - September.
Huge fruits. Inside are bright yellow sticky segments with a "Bubble-Gum" taste. Buy it already sliced, otherwise you will get stuck in the sap!
Longan (Lengkeng)
Season: June - September.
Bunches of sandy-brown balls. Inside is a transparent sweet pulp with a hard pit. Tastes like grapes with melon. Easy to peel.
Guava (Jambu Biji)
Season: Year-round.
Looks like a bumpy green apple. Inside is pink pulp with tiny seeds. Guava juice is great for Dengue fever!
Pineapple (Nanas)
Season: Year-round.
Pineapples in Bali are very sweet (especially yellow varieties). Do not buy green ones — they are for cooking.
White Mango (Wani)
Season: December - March.
A unique Balinese endemic! Brown skin, and snow-white pulp inside. Tastes like vanilla ice cream or custard.
Lychee (Leci)
Season: May – October.
A close relative of rambutan and longan, but with a brighter, perfumery taste. Less common and more expensive in Bali.
Yellow Watermelon (Semangka Kuning)
Season: Year-round.
In Bali, red watermelons are often watery, but bright yellow ones are incredibly sweet, honey-like, and almost always seedless!
Starfruit (Belimbing)
Season: Year-round.
Star-shaped fruit. Yellow fruits are sweet, green ones are sour (reminds of a mix of apple, cucumber, and gooseberry). Very juicy.
Bananas (Pisang)
Season: Year-round.
The tastiest are Pisang Susu (milk bananas): they are small, plump, and incredibly sweet. Green bananas are not always unripe, but locals mostly use them for frying.
Young Coconut (Kelapa Muda)
Season: Year-round.
Perfect natural isotonic! Ask specifically for Kelapa Muda (young coconut): it has sweet water and tender, jelly-like meat. Balinese use old coconuts for grating.
Where to buy?
No supermarkets (it is expensive there and everything is refrigerated). Buy at local street stalls (Pasar Buah) or morning markets.
