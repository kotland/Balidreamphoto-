“Dolphins at sunrise, hot spring in the jungle, dinner on the beach for 30K. Lovina is the Bali that forgot about tourists.”

Lovina is the northern coast of Bali. Black sand, calm sea, dolphins, hot springs and zero traffic jams. Pensioners, backpackers and those who want the cheapest and quietest Bali live here. The infrastructure is minimal, but it’s real Indonesia. The average age of expats is 40-60.

💻 Coworking spaces

There are no coworking spaces in Lovina. WiFi 3-10 Mbps. For remote work: this is not the best choice. Canggu (3 hours), Ubud (2.5 hours).

🌱 Vegan places

There are no separate vegan restaurants in Lovina. At warungs you can order tempeh, tofu, and vegetables. For plant-based cafe: Ubud (2.5 hours).

🏄 Surfing

There is no surfing in Lovina - north coast, calm sea, no waves. Nearest: Medevi (Medewi, 2 hours west) - long left point break.

🛍 Shopping

There is practically no shopping. Small souvenirs, sarongs, figurines. For shopping: Seminyak (3 hours) or Ubud (2.5 hours).

👕 Laundries

Hotel laundries
Most guesthouses do laundry for 5-7K/kg. There are few private laundries.

🎉 Clubs and nightlife

There is no nightlife. Several bars on the shore close at 22-23. Warung Music - live music in the evenings. It's a village and it's beautiful.

📍 Banjar · 15 min from Lovina · Donation

The temple on the lake is a postcard view of Bali. On the road from Lovina to the south.

Hindu temple with unique stone carvings. Northern Balinese style.
📍 Sangsit · 20 min · Free

🇷🇺 Russian community

There are practically no Russians in Lovina. There is no separate community. For Russian communication: Canggu or Ubud.

🎒 Kindergartens and schools

There are no international schools in Lovina. There are local schools (Indonesian language). For expat families: not suitable for long-stay with school-aged children.

🛵 Transport

• Scooter: 400K-600K/month (roads are good, no traffic)
• Grab/Gojek: does not work in Lovina
• To the airport: 3-3.5 hours, 500-700K (transfer through the mountains)
• To Ubud: 2.5 hours
• To Munjuk: 45 min
• Perama Bus: shuttle service to Ubud, Kutu, Sanur - from 100K

🤫 Secret of the area
Sekumpul Waterfall (1 hour from Lovina) is the most beautiful waterfall in Bali. Two streams in the jungle, local guides lead you down steps through banana plantations. There are few tourists because it is far from the south. Worth every minute of the journey.
⚠️ Cons
Very far from everything (airport 3+ hours). Poor WiFi. Few restaurants and shops. No nightlife. No gyms. No coworking spaces. Beaches with black sand (some don't like it). Rainy season - roads through the mountains are dangerous. Little medicine.
