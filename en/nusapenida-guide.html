“Rocks fall into the turquoise ocean, a manta ray swims beneath me, T-Rex Beach is like from another planet. Nusa Penida is Bali on steroids.”

Nusa Penida is an island 30 minutes by speedboat from Sanur. The most dramatic landscapes of Bali: cliffs, turquoise water, manta rays, Kelingking Beach. The island is developing quickly, but is still wild: bad roads, minimal infrastructure, but incredible beauty. For day trips or adventurous long-stays.

💻 Coworking spaces

There are no coworking spaces in Nusa Penida. WiFi in the cafe is 5-15 Mbps (unstable). 4G coverage is improving, but it's unreliable for video calls. For remote work: stay in Bali, go to Penida for the weekend.

🏄 Surfing

There are spots on Nusa Penida, but only for experienced ones:

🤿 Diving

Manta Point
Manta Ray cleaning station. Huge manta rays (span up to 5m) circle around you. One of the best dive sites in the world!
📍 South Penida · Depth 10-18m
Crystal Bay
Mola mola (ocean sunfish) from July to October! Crystal visibility, currents.
📍 West Penida · Depth 5-35m
Toyapakeh Wall
Wall with soft corals, giant gorgonians. Strong currents = lots of fish.
📍 Toyapakeh · Depth 5-40m
Manta Bay
Another point with manta rays. Less crowded than Manta Point.

📍 Toyapakeh
Sanctum Dive Nusa Penida

⚠️ The currents on Penida are strong. Be sure to dive with an experienced guide. Not for beginners without an instructor.

🌋 Volcanoes and mountains nearby

There are no volcanoes on Nusa Penida (it is a limestone island). View of Gunung Agung from the coast - amazing! For climbing: return to Bali.

🌾 Rice terraces nearby

There are no rice terraces on Nusa Penida - dry limestone landscape, seaweed. For terraces: Ubud (Bali).

⛳ Golf

There is no golf on Nusa Penida. Nearest: Bali National (Nusa Dua, Bali).

🍓 Farms

🛍 Shopping

There is no shopping in Nusa Penida. Small souvenirs at attractions. For shopping: Bali.

🛒 Grocery stores

Minimarket (Alfamart)
Several locations in Toyapakeh and Ped. Basic set: water, snacks, noodles.

There are no imported products. Everything is basic. For serious shopping: Bali.

👕 Laundries

Hotel laundries
Most guesthouses do laundry. 5-8K/kg. There is no self-service.

🎉 Clubs and nightlife

There is no nightlife. In the evening - dinner in the warung, beer, stars. For parties: go back to Bali.

🇷🇺 Russian community

There are almost no Russians permanently living on Nusa Penida. There are many Russian tourists on day trips. For the community: Bali (Canggu, Ubud).

🎒 Kindergartens and schools

There are no international schools or kindergartens for expats on Nusa Penida. The island is not suitable for families with school-aged children.

🛵 Transport

• Scooter: 70-100K/day (roads are BAD - potholes, dirt roads, hills. Experience required!)
• Grab/Gojek: doesn't work
• Speedboat from Bali: from Sanur, 200-300K, 30 min. Several flights per day.
• Day tour with driver: 500-700K/day (recommended - roads are dangerous)
• Car rental with driver: 600-800K/day

⚠️ The roads in Nusa Penida are the most dangerous in Bali. Steep descents, lack of fences, holes. If you have no experience on a scooter - take a car with driver.

🤫 Secret of the area
Peguyangan Waterfall is not a waterfall, but a 700-step staircase to a sacred spring in the rock. Along the way there are blue pools with salt water at different levels of the rock. Few people come - but this is one of the most magical places in all of Bali. Come early in the morning.
⚠️ Cons
Very bad roads (one of the worst in Indonesia). Dangerous descents to the beaches (hundreds of steps, no guardrails). Weak WiFi. Few shops and restaurants. No medicine (evacuation to Bali). Strong currents in the sea mean swimming is not possible everywhere. During the rainy season, roads wash out. There are a lot of tourists at Kelingking 10:00-15:00.
